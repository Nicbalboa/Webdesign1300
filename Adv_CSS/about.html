The FA20 engine in the 2017 Toyota 86 is a 2.0-liter, four-cylinder, naturally aspirated boxer engine. It was developed in partnership with Subaru and is part of the Subaru/Toyota "global engine" family. Here's a breakdown of its key specs and features:

Key Specifications:

  • Displacement: 2.0 liters (1998cc)
  • Configuration:Boxer (horizontally opposed)
  • Fuel System: Direct fuel injection (D-4S)
  • Ratio: 12.5:1
  • Max Horsepower: 205 hp at 7,000 RPM
  • Max Torque: 156 lb-ft at 6,400 RPM>
  • Bore x Stroke: 86.0 x 86.0 mm
  • Redline: 7,400 RPM
  • Valvetrain: DOHC (Dual Overhead Cam), 16 valves (4 per cylinder)
  • Fuel Type: Regular unleaded gasoline

Engaging Driving Experience

The 2017 Toyota 86 was engineered with enthusiasts in mind. Its lightweight body and balanced handling make it one of the most fun-to-drive cars in its price range. Whether you’re navigating tight corners or enjoying a winding road, the 86’s responsive steering, precise braking, and excellent grip give you a direct connection to the road. The 2.0-liter engine, while not overly powerful, offers the perfect blend of spirited performance and fuel efficiency.


Engine features

  • Boxer Design: The horizontally opposed cylinder layout contributes to a low center of gravity, which improves handling and stability.
  • D-4S System: The combination of direct and port fuel injection allows for better fuel efficiency and performance.
  • Variable Valve Timing (VVT): The intake cam is equipped with Toyota's VVT system, optimizing performance and efficiency across different RPM ranges.
  • Cooling and Lubrication: A sophisticated cooling system ensures that the engine maintains optimal operating temperatures even during spirited driving. The oil system is designed to keep the engine lubricated under various driving conditions.

Engine's Role in the 2017 Toyota 86

The FA20 engine plays a central role in the 86's character as a lightweight, agile, rear-wheel-drive sports car. Paired with either a 6-speed manual or a 6-speed automatic transmission, the engine provides a balance of performance and efficiency, allowing the 86 to be both a fun weekend toy and a daily driver. Its relatively modest power output gives it a more "pure" sports car feel, with great throttle response and the ability to rev freely to its high redline.
